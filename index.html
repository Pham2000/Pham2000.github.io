  • System Integration: Cybersecurity in U.S. Water and Wastewater Systems

    The project "System Integration: US Water and Wastewater System" is a comprehensive initiative aimed at safeguarding the critical infrastructure of water and wastewater systems (WWS) across the United States. These systems are vital components of the nation's infrastructure, providing essential services such as safe drinking water provision and wastewater treatment. Recognizing the significance of these systems to national security, public health, and environmental well-being, the project focuses on enhancing their resilience against potential threats, particularly cyber attacks. At the heart of the project lies the integration of Supervisory Control and Data Acquisition (SCADA) systems with existing water and wastewater infrastructure. SCADA systems are pivotal in monitoring and controlling various processes within WWS, ensuring efficient operation and compliance with regulatory standards. However, their integration also introduces vulnerabilities, making them potential targets for cyber threats that could disrupt operations and pose significant risks to society and the environment. The project aims to address these vulnerabilities by developing and implementing robust cybersecurity measures tailored specifically to SCADA-integrated WWS. Through a combination of advanced technologies, best practices, and strategic partnerships, the project seeks to mitigate cyber risks and enhance the overall security posture of water and wastewater infrastructure nationwide.
